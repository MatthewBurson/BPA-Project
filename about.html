What are Greenhouse Gases?

Greenhouse Gases is a gas that contributes to the greenhouse effect by absorbing infrared radiation, carbon dioxide and chlorofluorocarbons. Greenhouse Gases is a gas that absorbs and emits radiant energy within the thermal infrared range, causing the greenhouse effect. The primary greenhouse gases in Earth's atmosphere would be water vapor, carbon dioxide, methane, nitrus oxide, and ozone.

What is Carbon Footprint?

Carbon Footprint is the amount of carbon dioxide and other carbon compounds emitted due to the consumption of fossil fuels by a particular person, or groups, or nations. Carbon Footprint is bad for the population because the biosphere is where carbon resides in trees, and plants, and humans live but because of all of the carbon dioxide it gets harder to live.
