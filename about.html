What is Carbon Footprint?

Greenhouse gases (GHPs) are gases that increase the temperature of the Earth due to their absorption of infrared radiation. These gases are emitted from fossil fuel usage in electricity, in heat and transportation, as well as being emitted as byproducts of manufacturing. The most common GHGs are carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and many fluorinated gases. A greenhouse gas footprint is the numerical quantity of these gases that a single entity emits. The calculations can be computed ranging from a single person to the entire world. Although some emissions are natural, the rate of which they are being produced has increased because of humans.
